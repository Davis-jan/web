Government Revenue & Expenditure Analysis

The report analyzes Kenya’s public fiscal performance (1999–2024) using data from the Central Bank of Kenya (CBK), visualized through a Looker dashboard. Findings show a persistent fiscal deficit, with government expenditure consistently exceeding revenue, relying heavily on Income Tax, VAT, and Excise Duty, making the economy vulnerable due to limited revenue diversification. Additionally, recurrent expenditure, mainly wages and salaries, significantly outweighs development spending, restricting investment in economic growth. Key challenges include high deficits, tax dependence, and spending imbalances, requiring better tax compliance, expenditure control, and revenue diversification for sustainable fiscal management.

Relationship between Public Debt and Fiscal Policy(Kenya)

This report analyzes Kenya’s public debt and fiscal policy (1999–2024) using Python for data analysis and visualization, with data from the Central Bank of Kenya (CBK). Findings show a persistent rise in domestic and external debt due to budget deficits, where expenditure outpaces revenue, leading to heavy borrowing. A T-test analysis confirms a significant relationship between debt and fiscal performance, highlighting Kenya’s reliance on borrowing rather than revenue growth. To ensure debt sustainability, the report recommends enhancing revenue generation, optimizing expenditure, and adopting Public-Private Partnerships (PPPs) to reduce fiscal dependency on loans.

Determinants of Life Expectancy

This dataset examines factors influencing life expectancy (1999–2024) across countries, focusing on mortality rates, healthcare spending, economic indicators, and disease prevalence. Analysis reveals that higher education, income, and healthcare investment significantly improve life expectancy, while high mortality rates, child deaths, and HIV/AIDS prevalence reduce it. Regression results confirm strong positive effects from schooling, income composition, and GDP, while adult mortality and under-five deaths have the most negative impact. To enhance life expectancy, policymakers should invest in education, healthcare, and economic growth while prioritizing disease prevention and child mortality reduction.

Kenya Road Accidents Analysis

This project analyzes road accident trends in Kenya using Python, identifying patterns across counties, time periods, and weekdays vs. weekends. Findings show that Nairobi, Nakuru, and Mombasa have the highest accident rates, with peak occurrences between 6 PM and 9 PM, aligning with rush hours. Fridays and Saturdays record the most accidents, likely due to increased travel and nightlife . Using Pandas, Matplotlib, and Seaborn, visualizations such as bar charts, time-series plots, and heatmaps highlight key trends, with speeding, reckless driving, and pedestrian errors emerging as top causes. These insights support data-driven road safety policies, improved traffic enforcement, and accident prevention strategies.
